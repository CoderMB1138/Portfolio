Who am I?

I'm originally from San Diego, California. In 2012, I completed my first Bachelor's Dregree in European History at a small Liberal Arts college in Santa Barbara, California.

I then began my Career as an English Language instructor for International Students in the United States.

Over the years, I taught hundreds of pupils from diverse countries like Japan, Brazil, Saudi Arabia, and Switzerland how to speak English. Together, we improved in Writing, Reading, Speaking, and Listening skills.

In 2017 I took a big leap and decided to join a Web Development Bootcamp hosted by UC San Diego. After successfully completing this intensive MERN stack course and then started making plans to continue my education in Computer Science.

In 2019, I took another HUGE leap and moved to Ulm, Germany and joined a second bachelor's degree program at the University of Applied Science at Ulm (Technische Hochschule Ulm).

In my time in Germany I also gained valuable international experience working at firms in the automotive IT industry such as Harman Becker and BMW.

In my free time I also enjoy reading history books, travelling, yoga, and hiking.
